Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What does Dual App Manager do?
A: Dual App Manager is a macOS workspace launcher for locally installed apps, website shortcuts, and account notes. It is designed for users who need separate work and personal contexts, including WeChat and WeCom workflows.
Q: Does Dual App Manager upload my apps, accounts, or notes?
A: No. Dual App Manager stores workspace data locally and does not upload your app list, account notes, website shortcuts, or personal data. See the Privacy Policy for details.
Q: Do I need to install the Helper?
A: No. The base app can manage local workspaces, add apps, open original apps, and launch websites without the optional Helper. The Helper is only for advanced local instance features.
Q: Does Dual App Manager include or distribute WeChat, WeCom, or any third-party app?
A: No. Dual App Manager does not include, download, sell, or redistribute third-party apps. It only works with apps already installed on your Mac and selected by you.
Q: Why can an advanced instance feature fail?
A: Compatibility can depend on macOS, the selected app version, app signing, system policies, and the optional Helper status. Dual App Manager should show local compatibility information when a feature is unavailable.

Reporting Issues

  1. Describe the problem clearly.
  2. Include your macOS version and Dual App Manager version.
  3. Name the app involved, such as WeChat, WeCom, a browser, or another local app.
  4. List the steps to reproduce the issue.
  5. Include whether the optional Helper is installed if the issue relates to advanced instance features.
  6. Attach a screenshot if it helps explain the problem.
