DockLocker Privacy Policy

DockLocker is a macOS utility for preventing accidental Dock movement on multi-display Macs. This privacy policy explains how DockLocker handles permissions, settings, and local information on your Mac.

Last Updated: June 25, 2026

Overview

DockLocker is a macOS utility for preventing accidental Dock movement on multi-display Macs. This privacy policy explains how DockLocker handles permissions, settings, and local information on your Mac.

Data Collection

DockLocker does NOT collect, transmit, sell, or share your personal data.

What DockLocker Accesses

  • Display information: DockLocker reads connected display names, identifiers, sizes, and arrangement so you can choose a target display.
  • Mouse movement near display edges: When Pin Dock or Prevent Dock Pop-Up is enabled, DockLocker uses macOS Accessibility event access to process mouseMoved events near Dock trigger edges and check whether your temporary unlock modifier key is held.
  • Dock and Spaces preferences: DockLocker reads local macOS Dock orientation and separate-Spaces settings so it can calculate supported trigger edges and show accurate setup guidance.
  • App preferences: Target display selection, lock status, and temporary unlock shortcut settings are stored locally on your Mac.
  • Dock auto-hide setting: If you use Hide Dock or Show Dock, DockLocker asks macOS System Events to toggle the Dock auto-hide setting. This action stays on your Mac and can be changed back by you at any time.

What DockLocker Does Not Do

  • It does not record, store, or upload your mouse movement history.
  • It does not capture screen contents, keystroke text, files, windows, or app content.
  • It does not monitor keyboard events for text entry, passwords, or app usage.
  • It does not use analytics, advertising SDKs, tracking technologies, accounts, or sign-in.

Permissions and Access

DockLocker requests macOS Accessibility permission only when a feature needs it. For Dock locking, the permission is used to process mouseMoved events near display-edge Dock trigger zones and to allow your temporary unlock modifier. DockLocker does not use Accessibility permission to read keyboard input, screen contents, files, or personal data.

DockLocker may also request macOS Automation permission for System Events if you use Hide Dock or Show Dock. This is used only to toggle the Dock auto-hide preference through macOS, not to control other apps or read their content.

Local Storage

DockLocker stores settings locally using macOS preferences. These settings remain on your device.

  • Preferences: selected display, temporary unlock modifier key, and related app settings
  • Runtime state: current lock status is kept in memory while the app is running

Network Use

DockLocker does not need network access for Dock locking. If you click a support email link or an external web page, that request is handled by Mail, your browser, or another system app.

Data Retention

  • Your local preferences remain on your Mac until you change them, reset them, or remove the app data.
  • Runtime state is cleared when the app quits.
  • Uninstalling the app may not automatically remove every local preference file. You can manually delete remaining app data from your Mac if needed.

Children's Privacy

DockLocker does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be indicated by updating the Last Updated date at the top of this page.

Your Control

Because DockLocker works locally on your Mac:

  • You choose whether to grant Accessibility permission.
  • You choose when Dock locking is enabled or disabled.
  • You can remove the app and its local preferences at any time.
