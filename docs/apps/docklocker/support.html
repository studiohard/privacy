Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What does DockLocker do?
A: DockLocker helps keep Dock activation tied to your chosen display on multi-display Macs.
Q: Why does DockLocker need Accessibility permission?
A: DockLocker needs Accessibility permission only when Dock locking is enabled. It processes mouse movement near display-edge Dock trigger zones and detects the temporary unlock modifier key. See the Privacy Policy for details.
Q: How do I temporarily unlock the Dock?
A: Hold the temporary unlock shortcut configured in Settings. The default is Option.
Q: Why does locking not work on my setup?
A: Check that Accessibility permission is enabled, at least two displays are connected, the target display is available, and the Dock position is supported by your current display arrangement.
Q: Does DockLocker change my system Dock settings?
A: Dock locking reads your Dock orientation and display setup without changing them. If you use Hide Dock or Show Dock, DockLocker asks macOS System Events to toggle the Dock auto-hide setting, and you can change it back at any time.

Troubleshooting

  1. Open System Settings and confirm DockLocker is enabled under Privacy & Security > Accessibility.
  2. Reconnect external displays, then use the app's refresh or recheck action.
  3. Confirm the selected target display is still connected.
  4. Try changing the temporary unlock shortcut if it conflicts with another app.
  5. Quit and reopen DockLocker if macOS display arrangement changes are not reflected.

Reporting Issues

  1. Describe the problem clearly.
  2. Include your Mac model and macOS version.
  3. List your display arrangement and Dock position.
  4. Include your DockLocker version and temporary unlock shortcut.
  5. Attach a screenshot if it helps explain the problem.
